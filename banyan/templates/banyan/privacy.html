Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information.

Information you provide us directly:

  • Your Facebook or Twitter login information/username, Banyan username, and e-mail address when you register for a Banyan account.

  • User Content (e.g., photos, clips, frames, comments and other materials) that you post to the Banyan Service.
  • Communications between you and us. For example, we may send you Banyan Service-related emails (e.g., account verification, changes/updates to features of the Banyan Service, technical and security notices).

Finding your friends on Banyan:

  • If you choose, you may be able to invite friends to provide input on your User Content. You may be able to do this by searching for other people with Banyan accounts through (i) your contacts list, (ii) third-party social media sites or (iii) through a search of names and usernames on Banyan.
  • If you choose to find your friends through your device's contacts list, then Banyan will access your contacts list to determine whether someone associated with your contact is using Banyan.
  • If you choose to find your friends through a third-party social media site, then you will be prompted to set up a link to the third-party service and you understand that any information that such service may provide to us will be governed by this Privacy Policy.
  • If you choose to find your friends through a search of names or usernames on Banyan then simply type a name to search and we will perform a search on the Banyan Service.

We collect and use this analytics information with analytics information from other Users so that it cannot reasonably be used to identify any particular individual User.

Log file information:

  • Log file information is automatically reported by your browser each time you make a request to access (i.e., visit) a web page or app. It can also be provided when the content of the webpage or app is downloaded to your browser or device.
  • When you use the Banyan Service, our servers automatically record certain log file information, including your web request, Internet Protocol ("IP") address, browser type, referring / exit pages and URLs, number of clicks and how you interact with links on the Banyan Service, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed, and other such information. We may also collect similar information from emails sent to our Users which then help us track which emails are opened and which links are clicked by recipients. The information allows for more accurate reporting and improvement of the Banyan Service.

Device identifiers:

  • When you use a mobile device like a phone or tablet to access the Banyan Service, we may access, collect, monitor, store on your device, and/or remotely store one or more "device identifiers." Device identifiers are small data files or similar data structures stored on or associated with your mobile device, which uniquely identify your mobile device. A device identifier may be data stored in connection with the device hardware, data stored in connection with the device's operating system or other software, or data sent to the device by Banyan.
  • A device identifier may deliver information to us or to a third party partner about how you browse and use the Banyan Service and may help us or others provide reports or personalized content and ads. Some features of the Banyan Service may not function properly if use or availability of device identifiers is impaired or disabled.

Metadata:

  • Metadata is usually technical data that is associated with User Content. For example, metadata can describe how, when and by whom a piece of User Content was collected and how that content is formatted.
  • Users can add or may have metadata added to their User Content including a hashtag (e.g., to mark keywords when you post User Content), geotag (e.g., to mark your location to User Content), comments or other data. This makes your User Content more searchable by others and more interactive. If you geotag your User Content, your latitude and longitude will be stored with the User Content and searchable (e.g., through a location or map feature) if your User Content is made public by you in accordance with your privacy settings.
